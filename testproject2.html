One could arguably say that the Playstation 4 is the most relevant console in the gaming industry, and with the new Playstation 5 release date announced I started wondering what changes could be done to the UI to improve its usability.

Technology and Resources used for this project:

Hands on, I decided to get in touch with Playstation 4 Users and asked them to let me know about what changes could be done to improve its usability, if there were any, as I wanted to know from them the exact pain points they suffered when using the UI.


Since I have not used the PS4 as much as I used its predecessors I knew that I had to gather as much feedback as possible. Moreover, I only had 2 weeks to present this project in front of my class and It was clear to me that I had to take a decision: While I'm a big fan of quantitative methods due to their reliability, It was clear to me that I lacked the necessary resources to research as much as I'd like to.

Therefore, I decided to focus on qualitative methods, speaking with PS4 users directly in form of "Guerrilla Interviews" and comments.

8 Users were interviewed and I received around 30 comments on a Gaming group where I asked for their experiences with the PS4 Operative System, otherwise called ORBIS.



If you would like to, you can check this and review the most relevant notes from the research.

In case you have checked my notes, you will see that I labeled each proposal from users with an S, M, or L. This letters stand for Small, Medium or Large and allowed me to discard those proposals that were impossible to complete.

Thanks to all of this wonderful feedback, I decided to tackle the following issues:

  • Game Centered UI instead of the actual Multimedia Center.

  • Multi Profile Feature on the fly. Now you need to go through multiple screens just to swap profiles.

  • Changes in the information architecture. Sections such as Downloads are difficult to find.

...

I took my first drawings and asked my fellow Gamers about their thoughts. I wanted to test the prototype more than anything but I wasn't able to find any prototyping tool that could allow me to map the Dualshock 4 controller. After speaking with my users and showing them the first prototype it was clear that I still had to:

  • Do some minor changes to the UI to differentiate it from the Switch UI.

  • Redistribute the lower Menu and make it easier to use.

Additionally, I kept asking for feedback to my beloved friends in the Gaming group, receiving wonderful compliments and feedback.



Since I was lucky enough to find the Sony PS4 Styleguide, I decided to fully commit to make my prototype look as legit as possible. To achieve this, I drew an exact copy of the PS4 icons with Sketch.

Additionally I wanted users to have a good idea of how the final product would look and work, therefore I decided to fully animate the prototype with After Effects.

I was able to find a good mockup with the Dualshock 4 controller included, allowing me to animate the controller mapping.

Finally, I decided to add sound effects. As you can imagine, my composition in After Effects was heavy, therefore I decided to use Premiere Pro to finish the video.
